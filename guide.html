Remixa — Full Guide

remixa.app

Drop in your clips.
Get back a batch of ready-to-post videos.

Remixa runs on your own computer — nothing uploads, nothing queues, nothing charges per render. Make as many videos as you want. Here's the whole flow, start to finish.

Setup · 2 minutes

Install it like any app

No editing software, no plugins, no accounts. Windows 10/11 or macOS — that's the whole requirement list.

Windows

  1. Download Remixa-Setup.exe (purchase email or remixa.app).
  2. Double-click, click through. No admin password needed.
  3. Open Remixa from the Start menu — it opens in your browser, running 100% on your PC.

Mac

  1. Download the .dmg, drag Remixa into Applications.
  2. Launch it — it opens in your browser, running locally.
First launch: right-click → Open, then confirm. macOS warns once because Remixa is an independent app, not notarized through Apple. One time, then never again.

First screen asks for your license key (it's in your receipt email). Paste, hit Activate, done — one key works on 2 devices.

Step 1

Drop in whole clips — don't cut anything

No pre-chopping, no separating hooks from bodies. Drop in your full clips (you talking, then showing something) and Remixa finds the hook by itself — it detects where the talking ends and the screen part begins.

How many videos per batch? Type any number up to 1,000 — the more clips you add, the more different they come out.

Step 2

Text goes on your hook — just your hook

One thing to know before you type: captions and overlays show during your hook only (the talking opener), never over the rest of the video. The editor says so right under the bar, and the bar itself only covers the hook.

  • Wordings — give one caption several wordings and each video picks one. Ten wordings = ten different-feeling videos, free.
  • Timing — "0% to 50%" = first half of the hook. Segments can overlap, touch, or trade places.
  • Styles — TikTok-style border, white box, clean text, or a Snapchat bar. Five fonts, any color. Drag to place, corner dot to resize.

Happy with your setup? Save it as a layout — name it once and every position, style, timing window and wording comes back with one click on any future batch. No redoing your text every time. (Layouts store your text segments; overlays are per-project.)

Step 3

Pick a mode — here's which one

BasicStart here. Your clip's own structure, hook then screen. Great for tutorials and demos.
Multi-clipKeeps your order, but swaps the screen parts between clips. Talking stays in place — the walkthrough still makes sense, the visuals vary.
Split ScreenTalking hook, then your reaction stacked over the screen. Needs clips that have a reaction-over-screen part — if yours don't, stick to Basic or Multi-clip.

Mix clips sets how much your clips cross over: Off = each video is one clip. Hooks = openers rotate, bodies stay whole. All = stays in order, mixes and matches face/screen clips across everything you added.

Step 4

Your logo or stickers, background gone in one click

Add any image as an overlay — logo, product shot, screenshot — drag it into place, set when it shows. "Remove background" cuts the subject out automatically, so a photo becomes a clean sticker. Text always renders on top.

Step 5

Music that starts exactly where you want

Drop songs in and slide each waveform — TikTok-style — until the part you want sits at the start marker. It plays out loud while you slide, and the ▶ button previews your pick. In the final videos the music ducks under your voice automatically: quieter while you talk, up when you're not.

No music? Skip it — outputs keep each clip's original audio.

Step 6

Every video comes out different — automatically

Each copy gets its own small tweaks: zoom, brightness, contrast, color temperature, speed, position. Combined with wording mix-and-match and clip mixing, no two videos in a batch look or read the same. Every effect has a toggle and a range in Settings if you want it stronger or off.

Step 7

Generate, watch, download

Hit the gold button. The log shows each video as it renders — on a normal laptop a video takes roughly 20–30 seconds. Preview them right in the app, download the keepers, or grab the whole batch as one zip with Download all.
